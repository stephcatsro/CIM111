HOW TO DETERMINE IF IT'S VINTAGE

After reading this webpage, you should be able to make an educated guess on when your garment is from.

Vintage Fashion Guild

TAGS & LABELS

Includes Origin

Tags including a city are probably an indication of a vintage item

Same Brand, Different Label

This can mean a brand just redesigned their label or it is from a specialty line, but sometimes when you recognize the name but not the label, it might be from another era!

Care Tag

Since 1971, the Federal Trade Commission has required manufacturers to add labels explaining how to care for the item, if it has a label explaining how to care for the garment, it its probably from after 1971

MATERIAL

Spandex

1980s

Common fabrics used in the 80's include Spandex, Velvet, and Velour

STITCHING

French/Unfinished Seams

The edges of these seams were either left untouched or turned into French Seams

Pinked Seams

Zig Zag pattern most commonly found on 1950's clothes

Serged Seams

1960s+ when sewing machines became widely available

NUMBERS

RN Numbers

RN numbers can be found on the tags on your garment. These numbers are unique to businesses who sell/manufacture clothes. This was issued by the FTC in 1959.
